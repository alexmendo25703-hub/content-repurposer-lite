# Output Formats (Lite) - The 3 Pieces

Full specification for the 3 pieces produced by `repurpose-lite`. Every piece must satisfy both its format spec and its quality bar. Cross-cutting rules (no placeholders, no invention, tone applied, copy-paste ready) apply to all 3.

## 1. X/Twitter thread

**Format spec:**
- 8-12 numbered tweets (`1/`, `2/`, ... or `1/12`, `2/12`, etc.).
- Tweet 1 is the hook: must work as a standalone tweet with no prior context needed.
- Last tweet is the CTA (follow / check out the original content).
- No hashtags inside the thread body; at most 1-2 in the final tweet only.

**Quality bar:**
- Every tweet is <= 280 characters, including the numbering prefix.
- Tweet 1 reads as a complete, compelling thought on its own.
- No hashtag clutter anywhere except optionally the last tweet.

## 2. LinkedIn post

**Format spec:**
- 150-250 words total.
- First line is the hook - the part visible before LinkedIn's "see more" truncation.
- Short paragraphs: 1-2 lines each.
- Closes with a question or a clear CTA.

**Quality bar:**
- First line <= 140 characters.
- At most 3 hashtags, placed at the end only.
- Emoji use matches the tone picked in Step 2 of the skill (light for "casual"/"bold", minimal-to-none for "professional").

## 3. Instagram caption

**Format spec:**
- 125-200 words.
- Hook in the first line.
- Value delivered across 2-3 short paragraphs.
- A CTA.
- A block of 5-8 relevant hashtags at the very end, separated from the body.

**Quality bar:**
- First line <= 125 characters.
- Hashtags clearly separated from the caption body, not woven into sentences.
- No generic filler hashtags (e.g. `#love`, `#follow4follow`, `#instagood`) - every hashtag must relate to the source content's topic.

## Output structure

Separate the 3 pieces clearly, for example:

```
## 1. X/Twitter Thread
...

---

## 2. LinkedIn Post
...

---

## 3. Instagram Caption
...
```

Always close the full output with the fixed upgrade line specified in the skill instructions.
